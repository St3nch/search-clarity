# r03 — SearchClarity First 5 Gig Report Strategy Review

> **Document type:** Strategy review and critique — not a final template or deliverable  
> **Scope:** SEO reports and visibility deliverables only  
> **Project:** SearchClarity / Neon Ronin  

---

## 1. Executive Summary

**Are these the right first 5 gigs?** Mostly yes, but in the wrong order and with one that should not launch yet.

**Blunt verdicts:**

The Etsy Listing Visibility Audit is the right anchor gig. It has the clearest buyer problem, the most defensible professional framing, and the most natural upsell path. It should launch first and carry the public sample report.

The Etsy Keyword Research Pack is a trap. Standalone keyword research is the commodity that SearchClarity is trying to differentiate itself against. Launch it only as an add-on to the Audit, not as a standalone gig.

The Etsy Competitor Visibility Report is too complex for early launch. It requires more research judgment, carries higher ethical risk, and is better framed as a Standard/Premium tier of the Audit. Launch it later as a standalone only if buyers clearly want it.

The Etsy Title and Tag Rewrite is the most dangerous to brand positioning. Done right, it is a high-value implementation service. Done wrong, it is exactly what a $10 keyword goblin already sells. It should launch as a standalone but only after the Audit establishes the brand. Not in the first 3.

The Pinterest SEO Plan for Etsy Sellers does not belong in the first 5. It appeals to a different buyer at a different stage of sophistication. Launch it after Etsy SEO is validated.

**Summary:**

| Priority | Action |
|---|---|
| Launch first | Etsy Listing Visibility Audit — solo, with public sample report |
| Launch second | Etsy Title and Tag Rewrite — as an add-on and standalone |
| Launch third | Etsy Keyword Research Pack — as an add-on only, not standalone |
| Delay | Etsy Competitor Visibility Report — convert to Audit upsell tier |
| Delay significantly | Pinterest SEO Plan — launch after Etsy SEO has traction |

**Biggest risk to quality:** Rushing to fill all five gig slots before the first one is proven. One excellent, specific, well-reviewed gig beats five mediocre ones.

**Biggest risk to buyer trust:** The Keyword Research Pack landing on the same page as a $5 competitor. If it looks like a spreadsheet dump, the Audit's professional positioning collapses.

## 2. Review of the Current First-Gig Strategy

**Current decision:** Launch first with the Etsy Listing Visibility Audit.

**Verdict: Correct. Do not change this.**

### Is it clear enough for Fiverr buyers?

Yes, with caveats. "Listing Visibility Audit" is understood by Etsy sellers who have been selling for a while. For very new sellers, "Why are my listings not showing up?" may be a more resonant hook in the gig description. The word "audit" signals professional review, which is intentional and correct.

### Is it broad enough to attract demand?

Etsy has millions of active sellers. Listing visibility anxiety is universal. The buyer pool is large. This is not a narrow niche offer.

### Is it specific enough to avoid sounding generic?

The offer is specific. The risk is in the execution. If the sample report looks like a keyword list with a PDF wrapper, it will feel generic regardless of what the gig description says. The specificity must be demonstrated in the sample — not just described.

### Does it support a professional sample report?

Yes. Better than any other gig in the list. A listing-by-listing audit naturally shows structured thinking, specific evidence, and clear recommendations. The Keyword Research Pack cannot show that without looking like a spreadsheet. The Competitor Report has ethical complexity. The Rewrite is output-only with no reasoning visible. The Audit is the only gig where a sample report can demonstrate both the research depth and the reasoning quality simultaneously.

### Does it create natural upsells?

Yes. After an audit, the natural next steps are:
- "Get the rewrites done" → Title and Tag Rewrite
- "Show me my keyword opportunities" → Keyword Research Pack as add-on
- "Show me what my competitors are doing" → Competitor Report add-on
- "Help me with Pinterest traffic" → Pinterest Plan (later)

The Audit is the front door to everything else.

### What would make this offer fail?

- Sample report that looks AI-generated or generic
- Promising too much in the gig description and under-delivering in the report
- Delivery time that is too short to credibly claim human review (under 2 days is suspicious for a professional audit)
- Pricing too low to signal expertise (under $75 for Basic positions it as a cheap gig)
- No public sample report when competitors have one

### 4.3 Tables and Analysis Elements

| Table | Value to buyer | Risk / caveat |
|---|---|---|
| Listing diagnosis table (all listings at a glance) | High — buyer sees the full picture before the detail | Keep brief. Overly complex diagnosis tables feel like padding. |
| Title structure table (current vs recommended, annotated) | Very high — this is the most tangible output | Every change must include a one-line reason. Unannotated rewrites look like keyword stuffing. |
| Tag coverage table (current tags vs gap keywords) | High | Must label overlaps vs gaps clearly. Risk: looks like a keyword dump if not accompanied by reasoning. |
| Keyword opportunity table | High for Standard/Premium | Must include data source, estimated volume, competition level, and seasonal note per keyword. Volume figures must be labeled as estimated. |
| Priority action table (Do first / Do next / Test later / Avoid) | Very high — this is what buyers remember | Keep it implementable. One sentence per action. No jargon. |
| Competitor observation table | Medium — high risk if done poorly | Only include if observations are clearly from manual search, not tool estimates. Never name specific competitor shops. Describe observable patterns only. |

### 4.4 Sample Report Fit

**Yes — this is the right gig for the public sample report.**

No other gig in the list can demonstrate reasoning quality as clearly as a listing audit. The Keyword Research Pack would look like a spreadsheet. The Competitor Report has ethical complexity. The Rewrite is output-only. The Audit is the only format where the thinking process is visible alongside the deliverable.

The sample report should be based on a fictional shop (Maplewood Candle Co. per r02 spec) and clearly labeled on every page. It should be made public as a partial preview (3 pages) with the full version gated behind email signup or purchase.

### 4.5 Package Strategy

**Proposed pricing:**

| Tier | Price | Listings | Turnaround | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | $75 | 3 listings | 3 business days | PDF audit report, listing diagnosis, title/tag recommendations with reasoning, priority action plan, caveats |
| Standard | $145 | 5 listings | 4 business days | Everything in Basic + keyword opportunity table, alternative paths, appendix keyword reference |
| Premium | $245 | 8 listings | 5 business days | Everything in Standard + keyword spreadsheet, 1-page priority action checklist, competitor observation notes |

**Verdict on proposed pricing:** The Basic at $75 is correct. It is high enough to signal professional value and low enough to attract first-time buyers. The Standard at $145 is slightly high for Fiverr with no reviews — consider $125 for the first 3–5 orders to build social proof, then move to $145. The Premium at $245 is appropriate and should not be discounted.

**Critical pricing rule:** Do not drop below $65 for any package at any time. Sub-$65 signals the buyer is getting a keyword dump, not a professional report.

### 4.6 What to Avoid

- Any language in the gig description that promises ranking improvements or sales increases
- A sample report that reads like it was generated from a template prompt
- Delivery in under 2 business days for any tier (signals no real human review happened)
- Tags in the gig that attract buyers looking for "13 Etsy tags" style services
- Keyword tables with volume numbers and no source labels
- Report sections that are obviously copy-pasted from a generic SEO template
- Recommendations that could apply to any Etsy shop, not the specific listings reviewed
- Offering revisions on the analysis itself (this creates scope creep; revisions should only cover misunderstandings or missing information, not "I disagree with your findings")

## 5. Gig 2 Review — Etsy Keyword Research Pack

### Should it be standalone or an add-on?

**Add-on only at launch.** This is the right call for several reasons:

The standalone keyword research market on Fiverr is saturated with $5–$15 gigs delivering spreadsheets. A $55+ standalone keyword pack will be compared directly to those offerings in search results. Without the Audit's established credibility and reviews, there is no visible differentiator.

As an add-on to the Audit, it is positioned as "deeper keyword data for buyers who want more than the audit's keyword findings section." That framing is defensible and avoids the keyword dump comparison.

### How do we avoid making it look like a keyword dump?

Three requirements:

**1. Cluster the keywords, never just list them.** Raw alphabetical or volume-sorted keyword lists look like dump output. Organized keyword packs group terms by search intent type: buyer-ready (high intent, occasion + recipient), research-phase (browsing, no clear intent), seasonal (demand spikes), and long-tail personalization (name-based, profession-based modifiers).

**2. Include a one-paragraph interpretation per cluster.** Not just "here are 15 keywords about nurse gifts." Instead: "This cluster targets buyers searching for occasion-specific nurse gifts. The volume is moderate but the intent is high — buyers in this cluster know what they want and are comparing products, not ideas. Competition is manageable for a shop with fewer than 200 sales."

**3. Label every volume estimate.** Every number needs a source tag. Never present an eRank estimate as fact. Never present Google Trends data as Etsy search volume.

### Should it be a PDF, spreadsheet, or both?

**Spreadsheet as the primary deliverable, PDF interpretation guide as the companion.** The spreadsheet is what buyers will actually use when writing listings. The PDF is what makes it look like a professional research product instead of a data export.

### How should keywords be grouped?

| Cluster | Description |
|---|---|
| Primary buyer-intent terms | High intent, occasion + recipient modifiers, ready-to-purchase signals |
| Long-tail personalization terms | Name, profession, occasion-year, relationship-based variants |
| Seasonal terms | Peak month noted; demand curve noted |
| Competitive gap terms | Keywords where top competing listings are weak or absent |
| Avoid list | Terms that appear high-volume but are too broad, trademark-risky, or historically underperforming |

### Package structure

| Tier | Price | Keywords delivered | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | $55 | 25 keywords across 3 clusters | Spreadsheet with volume estimates, source labels, intent level; short interpretation note per cluster |
| Standard | $95 | 50 keywords across 5 clusters | Everything in Basic + seasonal notes, personalization variant suggestions, avoid list |
| Premium | $175 | 75 keywords across 5+ clusters | Everything in Standard + PDF interpretation guide, recommended keyword application map (which keywords go in title vs tags vs description) |

**Verdict on proposed pricing:** Correct. Do not discount below $55 for Basic. At that price point the positioning holds if the deliverable is clearly superior to a $15 dump. At $45 or below it collapses.

**Tool and data caveats that must appear in every deliverable:**

- Volume figures are estimates from third-party tools (eRank, Etsy Marketplace Insights). They are not guaranteed search volumes.
- Etsy search data does not have a public API. All volume estimates are modeled, not measured.
- Seasonal patterns are based on historical trend data and may shift.
- These keywords are starting points for testing, not guaranteed traffic drivers.

## 6. Gig 3 Review — Etsy Competitor Visibility Report

### Should this launch early?

**No. Delay it.**

The Competitor Visibility Report is the most research-intensive and ethically complex gig in the list. It requires the analyst to:

- Identify relevant competing listings without defining "competitor" in a way that is wrong for the buyer's specific situation
- Make observations about competing shops without implying the buyer should copy them
- Avoid any recommendation that could encourage trademark infringement, style copying, or IP-adjacent behavior
- Explain why a competitor ranks well without having access to their actual performance data

All of this requires more research judgment than the Listing Audit and more delivery consistency than is achievable in early operations. One bad competitor report — where the buyer uses the findings to copy a competitor's protected elements — creates a significant reputational risk.

### Is it better as an upsell to the Visibility Audit?

**Yes. Fold it into the Premium tier of the Audit.**

At Premium, add a "Competitor Observations" section to the Listing Audit. This is not a full competitor report — it is a structured observation of what the top-ranking listings for the buyer's target keywords are doing in terms of title structure, tag patterns, and product positioning. It does not name specific shops. It describes observable patterns.

This framing is:
- Safer ethically
- Easier to deliver consistently
- More useful to the buyer (because it is tied to their specific listing opportunities, not a generic competitor study)

### How to keep this ethical

| What is acceptable | What is not acceptable |
|---|---|
| Observing title and tag patterns in top-ranking listings | Naming specific competitor shops in the report |
| Noting that top-ranking listings use occasion + recipient modifiers | Suggesting the buyer copy another seller's title structure verbatim |
| Identifying keyword gaps where top listings are weak | Reverse-engineering a competitor's sales estimates and presenting them as fact |
| Describing the competitive density for a target keyword | Recommending the buyer undercut a specific competitor's pricing |
| Noting the personalization approaches common in top listings | Any analysis that could be used to target or harm a specific seller |

### Analysis areas

| Analysis area | Include? | Why / why not |
|---|---|---|
| Title structure patterns in top-ranking listings | Yes | Observable from Etsy search. High value to buyer. |
| Tag patterns visible in public listings | Yes | Visible in listing source. Legitimate observation. |
| Personalization angle frequency | Yes | Visible and useful for positioning. |
| Estimated competitor sales/revenue | No | Model-based estimates. Never present as fact. Creates false competitive picture. |
| Competitor shop names | No | No reason to name them. Creates unnecessary risk. |
| Competitor pricing analysis | Optional, brief | Only if buyer asks and only as market context, not a pricing recommendation. |
| Trend observations (are top listings seasonal?) | Yes | Visible from listing dates, review dates, seasonal modifiers in titles. |

### Pricing verdict

| Tier | Price | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | $85 | Too low if standalone. As an add-on to Audit Basic, $45 is appropriate. |
| Standard | $145 | Correct if standalone with a strong deliverable. |
| Premium | $245 | Correct. |

**Blunt verdict:** Do not launch this as a standalone gig until the Audit has 10+ reviews. Build it as a Premium Audit inclusion first. Run 5 Premium deliveries. If buyers consistently ask for more competitor depth, spin it into a standalone.

## 7. Gig 4 Review — Etsy Title and Tag Rewrite

### Should this exist early?

**Yes, but as the second gig — not the first.**

The Rewrite is the natural next step after an Audit. Buyers who get an Audit and want someone to just do the implementation are a predictable conversion path. The gig exists to serve that buyer.

The problem is sequencing. If the Rewrite launches before the Audit has established credibility, it will be evaluated on Fiverr against $5–$15 "I will write your Etsy tags" gigs with no distinguishing context. It will lose.

After the Audit has reviews, the Rewrite can reference those reviews as proof of quality. "Same team that does the full visibility audit, now doing implementation" is a defensible position.

### Does it cheapen the brand?

**Only if priced and delivered wrong.**

The cheapening risk comes from two failure modes:

1. **Priced too low.** At $35–$45, it is indistinguishable from a keyword dump gig.
2. **Delivered without reasoning.** If the buyer gets new titles and tags with no explanation of why the changes were made, it looks like an automated output.

Both risks are solvable. Price it at $65+ for Basic. Include a one-line reasoning note for every title and every tag change. Format it as a revision document, not a raw list.

### Should it be a standalone gig or an add-on?

**Both.** Buyers who discovered SearchClarity through the Audit should be able to add a Rewrite as an order. Buyers who found the Rewrite gig directly (without buying an Audit first) should be able to order it standalone — but the gig description should mention that the Audit is available if they want deeper research first.

### Reasoning requirement

**Every rewrite must include a reason.** This is non-negotiable for brand positioning.

Format:

> **Old title:** "Smells Like Retirement — Candle Gift"  
> **New title:** "Personalized Retirement Gift Candle — Funny Nurse Retirement Candle for Women, RN Gift"  
> **Why:** Moved buyer-intent phrase to front position. Added profession modifier (nurse) and gender signal for algorithm and buyer filtering. Removed brand phrase from title (better suited to shop description).

Without this reasoning, it is a keyword dump with a nicer PDF.

### Listings per package

| Tier | Price | Listings covered | Turnaround | Includes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basic | $65 | 3 listings | 2 business days | Revised title + full 13 tags per listing, one-line reason per change, formatted as copy-paste-ready document |
| Standard | $110 | 5 listings | 3 business days | Everything in Basic + description opening paragraph per listing, keyword note explaining intent behind changes |
| Premium | $175 | 8 listings | 4 business days | Everything in Standard + personalization hook suggestion per listing, seasonal modifier notes |

**Verdict on proposed pricing:** The proposed Basic of $35–$45 is too low. Move it to $65 minimum. The Standard and Premium ranges are close — the top end of the proposed ranges is correct ($85 Standard → move to $110; $145 Premium → move to $175).

### Risks and prevention

| Risk | How to prevent it |
|---|---|
| Buyer expects the rewrite to fix all visibility problems | State clearly in gig description and in the deliverable: "Title and tag optimization is one factor in Etsy visibility. This service does not address photography, pricing, shop health, or listing conversion." |
| Buyer receives rewrites that look identical across multiple listings | Require listing URLs in intake. Each rewrite must be specific to that listing's product and niche. No copy-paste across listings. |
| Buyer uses rewrites that violate Etsy policy (keyword stuffing, prohibited terms) | Include a brief policy reminder in the deliverable: avoid repeating the same keyword multiple times across title + tags, avoid trademarked terms. |
| Rewrite looks like AI output | Human-review every deliverable. Reasoning notes must be written in plain English, not template language. |
| Scope creep: buyer wants unlimited revisions | Define revision scope clearly: one revision round per order, limited to factual errors or misunderstood product details. Not "I want different keywords." |

## 8. Gig 5 Review — Pinterest SEO Plan for Etsy Sellers

### Should this be in the first 5?

**No. Remove it from the launch lineup.**

### Why?

**Different buyer, different research method, different validation requirement.**

The Pinterest SEO buyer is a more sophisticated seller who has already solved basic Etsy visibility and is now looking for off-platform traffic. They understand pins, boards, and Pinterest search — or are specifically trying to. This is not the same buyer who searches "why is my Etsy listing not showing up."

Launching a Pinterest service before the Etsy SEO core is validated splits the brand positioning, requires a different research methodology (Pinterest Trends, pin content strategy, board structure), and introduces delivery risk in a service area that has not been tested yet.

### What attracts a different buyer?

Etsy SEO buyers are reacting to a specific pain point: low visibility on Etsy search. Pinterest SEO buyers are proactively investing in traffic growth. These two buyer mindsets produce different Fiverr searches, different price sensitivity, and different expectations for what a "result" looks like.

If a Pinterest buyer finds SearchClarity through the Etsy gigs and asks about Pinterest, the right answer is: "We can do a Pinterest plan as a custom order — contact us." That keeps the revenue without requiring a launch-day gig.

### When should this gig launch?

After the Etsy Listing Visibility Audit has at least 10 reviews and the Rewrite gig is live. At that point, there is enough credibility to offer a Pinterest plan to buyers who come through organic Fiverr search for Pinterest services, and enough operational capacity to deliver it consistently.

### What should the deliverable include when it does launch?

- Board structure recommendations (how many boards, what to name them, which to prioritize)
- Keyword strategy for pin titles and descriptions (using Pinterest Trends, not Etsy tools)
- Pin content brief: what types of pins to create, what buyer journey stage each targets
- Seasonal calendar: when to start pinning for each major seasonal moment, given that Pinterest demand runs 3–6 months ahead of Google
- Cross-listing strategy: which Etsy listings map to which boards and why
- Caveats: Pinterest traffic is slow to build, analytics take time, no traffic guarantees

**Proposed pricing verdict:**

| Tier | Price | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Basic | $95 | Appropriate |
| Standard | $165 | Appropriate |
| Premium | $275 | Appropriate |

The pricing is not the problem. The sequencing is.

## 11. Recommended Report Standards Across All Gigs

| Standard | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Every recommendation must reference the specific listing, keyword, or pattern it addresses | Proves human review. Generic advice is indistinguishable from AI output. |
| Every volume or performance figure must have a source label and an "estimated" qualifier | Protects against misrepresentation. Etsy search data is estimated, not measured. |
| Every report must include a caveats section stating: no ranking guarantees, no sales guarantees, and what SEO cannot fix | Legal and ethical standard. Builds trust by being honest about limits. |
| Every report must include a "how we reached these recommendations" section or equivalent | This is the SearchClarity differentiator. Visible reasoning is the product. |
| Every report must end with a prioritized action plan | Buyers must be able to act without asking follow-up questions. |
| No report should include competitor shop names | Ethical and legal risk. Describe patterns, not shops. |
| No report should include recommendations that reference brand names, celebrity names, trademarked phrases, sports teams, or IP-adjacent content | Buyer liability. If a buyer follows a recommendation and gets a listing removed, SearchClarity gets blamed. |
| Delivery time must be realistic for genuine human review | Basic: minimum 2 business days. Standard: minimum 3. Premium: minimum 4. Rushing signals no real review happened. |
| Revision scope must be defined before delivery | Revisions for factual errors or misunderstood product details only. Not for "I want different opinions." |
| All reports must be human-reviewed before delivery, even if drafting is assisted | Fiverr's policy and SearchClarity's positioning both require this. AI drafting is acceptable. AI-only delivery is not. |

## 12. Buyer Intake Review

### Required for all orders

- Shop URL (required — cannot do any listing work without it)
- Up to 5 specific listing URLs they want prioritized (prevents scope creep and focuses the work)
- Main product niche or category in one sentence (helps the analyst orient quickly)
- Main buyer persona if known: "I sell to nurses, teachers, and retirees looking for gifts"
- Any keywords they are already trying to rank for (useful context, not a direction setter)
- Anything they want to avoid (brand names, certain styles, certain markets)

### Needed for listing audits

- Are the listings handmade, POD, or digital? (Affects how we frame production partner disclosure notes)
- Are any listings personalized? If yes, which ones?
- Any recent changes they made to listings? (Helps avoid auditing a title they just changed)

### Needed for keyword research

- Primary niche (gift type, product category, target recipient)
- Geographic focus: US only, UK, EU, or international?
- Seasonal priority: is there a key season or event they are targeting?
- What they have already tried (prevents delivering keywords they already use)

### Needed for competitor reports

- What Etsy search terms they think of as their primary competition
- Whether they want the analysis focused on their specific product type or the broader niche
- Confirmation that they understand this is a pattern observation report, not a copy-from-competitors guide (set expectations upfront)

### Needed for title and tag rewrites

- Current titles and tags for each listing (listing URLs are sufficient if the analyst can pull them)
- Are there any phrases or words they want included? (Brand modifiers, material names, location modifiers)
- Are there any phrases or words they want excluded?
- Personalization format if relevant: "Name goes in brackets like [Name]" — this must appear in the rewrite

### Needed for Pinterest plans

- Current Pinterest account URL if they have one
- Etsy listings they want to prioritize for Pinterest traffic
- Content creation capacity: can they create new pin graphics, or are they limited to repurposing existing listing photos?
- Primary seasonal moment they want to target first

### Questions that are unnecessary or overwhelming for Fiverr buyers

**Remove or simplify:**
- Anything asking for Google Analytics or Etsy stats access (most sellers will not share this and it creates a trust barrier)
- Questions about conversion rate, AOV, or revenue (out of scope for SEO reports)
- Questions about business goals beyond the immediate listing/keyword/Pinterest scope
- Questions about photography or product development (out of scope)
- More than 8 questions total in any intake form — Fiverr buyers abandon long intake forms

- Any tool that accesses Etsy data via methods that violate the Etsy API ToS

## 14. Sample Report Strategy

### Is the Etsy Listing Visibility Audit the right sample report to make public?

**Yes. Unambiguously.**

The audit format is the only one that shows both the research depth and the reasoning quality in the same document. The keyword pack shows data without reasoning. The rewrite shows output without research. The competitor report has ethical constraints. The Pinterest plan requires a different buyer to evaluate it.

The Audit sample report is what buyers will use to answer the question: "Is this service worth paying for?" It must answer that question clearly and immediately.

### Should mini-samples be made for other gigs?

**Not at launch.** One excellent sample report is more valuable than five mediocre ones. After the Audit sample is complete and live, consider a 1–2 page "sample page" from the Rewrite deliverable (showing the before/after format with reasoning) as a Fiverr gallery image. That is sufficient.

### What should the public sample prove?

- That the analyst actually read the listings and understood them
- That the recommendations are specific to those listings, not generic advice
- That the reasoning is visible and follows from observable evidence
- That the report is honest about what it cannot guarantee
- That the buyer could act on the recommendations immediately

### What should the public sample avoid?

- Fake metrics, fake search volumes, or invented before/after results
- AI-patterned writing (long hedge phrases, generic transitions, filler qualifiers)
- Layout that looks like a Canva template rather than a professional document
- Any claim that could be read as a guarantee of results

### Full sample vs gated vs partial public?

**Recommendation:** 3-page public preview (cover + executive summary + 1 full listing audit page) available on Fiverr and SearchClarity website. Full 16-page sample gated behind email signup on SearchClarity.co. The preview earns attention. The full sample earns trust and captures email for follow-up.

### Fiverr gallery images

| Image | Content | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Image 1 (main) | Report cover with "SAMPLE REPORT" label visible. Clean, professional layout. SearchClarity wordmark. | First impression. No fake metrics, no ranking promises. |
| Image 2 | One listing audit page showing: current title (with problem annotations in a second color) → revised title (with reason). | Shows the core deliverable. Specificity visible at a glance. |
| Image 3 | Priority action plan table (Do first / Do next / Test later / Avoid). | Shows the report is implementable. Buyers can see what they will get before ordering. |

Do not use star ratings, fake "before/after" sales screenshots, or revenue claims in any gallery image.

## 15. Biggest Risks

| Risk | Severity | Why it matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sample report reads like AI output | High | Destroys the human-review positioning immediately. Single biggest early credibility risk. | Write in direct, specific, first-person professional voice. Every recommendation references a specific listing element. Have a second person read it before publishing. |
| Keyword Research Pack positioned as standalone gig too early | High | Will be compared to $5 competitors in Fiverr search. No differentiation without Audit credibility behind it. | Launch as add-on only. Do not create a standalone gig until 10+ Audit reviews exist. |
| Delivery time set too short | High | A 1-day turnaround for a "human-reviewed professional audit" is not credible. Fiverr buyers know this. | Minimum 2 business days for Basic. 3–4 for higher tiers. State this clearly in gig description. |
| Pricing too low on Rewrite gig | Medium-High | Sub-$65 Rewrite gig gets lumped in with keyword dump competitors regardless of quality. | Hold $65 as the floor. No exceptions. |
| Competitor Report launched before Audit brand is established | Medium | Ethical complexity and delivery difficulty before operational rhythm is set creates inconsistency risk. | Do not launch standalone until Audit has 10+ reviews. Fold competitor observations into Audit Premium first. |
| Scope creep on Rewrite revisions | Medium | Buyers who disagree with recommendations will request unlimited revisions. | Define revision scope clearly in gig description and in the deliverable. Revisions for errors, not for disagreements. |
| Tool volume estimates presented as facts | Medium-High | If a buyer acts on a volume estimate and gets zero traffic, they blame the report. Honest labeling is the only protection. | Every volume figure needs a source label and an "estimated" qualifier. No exceptions. |
| Pinterest gig launching too early | Medium | Splits brand, attracts a different buyer segment, adds operational complexity before core is validated. | Do not launch until Etsy SEO gigs have 15+ combined reviews. |
| Platform compliance (Fiverr) | Low-Medium | Fiverr requires disclosure of AI tool use when asked by clients. Not disclosing is a ToS violation. | Include in gig description: "This service is human-reviewed. Research and drafting may use AI-assisted tools." Do not claim no AI is used. |
