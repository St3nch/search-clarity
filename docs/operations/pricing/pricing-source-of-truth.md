## Pricing Principles

SearchClarity pricing should:

- avoid commodity positioning
- reflect buyer value, not only production time
- reflect human review, report quality, delivery polish, claim safety, and market positioning
- keep the Standard tier attractive if it remains the hero tier
- prevent unlimited revision/support exposure
- account for Fiverr's 20% fee
- account for buyer communication and revision/correction handling
- avoid pricing so low that buyers expect cheap keyword dumps
- avoid pricing so high that a new Fiverr account looks disconnected from visible marketplace comparisons

Agent-assisted production may improve margins later, but it should not push SearchClarity into commodity pricing.

## Fiverr Fee Reality

Fiverr takes 20% of each order.

Use this formula when evaluating candidate prices:

```text
estimated_net_before_taxes = buyer_price * 0.80
```

Example only:

| Buyer price | Approx SearchClarity revenue before taxes/expenses |
|---:|---:|
| $50 | $40 |
| $100 | $80 |
| $150 | $120 |
| $200 | $160 |

These examples are not approved package prices.

## When To Revisit Pricing

Revisit pricing when:

- manual Fiverr tier verification is complete
- Fiverr UI minimum prices are verified
- first sample PDF is complete
- Maplewood mock report reveals actual production time
- first 3-5 test orders or paid orders reveal actual production time
- revision volume is higher than expected
- buyer quality is poor
- competitor pricing changes materially
- SearchClarity adds stronger proof assets
- SearchClarity moves beyond Fiverr to direct sales
