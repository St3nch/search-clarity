## Purpose

This document defines the SearchClarity Etsy Listing Visibility Audit offer.

It is the source of truth for the first SearchClarity paid service before channel-specific copy is finalized.

Channel-specific versions, such as Fiverr gig setup, must reference this offer spec instead of inventing their own scope.

---

## Offer Summary

The Etsy Listing Visibility Audit is a human-reviewed SEO and marketplace visibility report for Etsy sellers.

The audit reviews visible listing/shop factors and gives specific recommendations to improve listing clarity, keyword coverage, buyer understanding, and marketplace readiness.

The audit does not guarantee rankings, traffic, sales, revenue, or Etsy placement.

---

## Report Language Standard

The report should sound professional and clear.

Use normal-person language wherever possible.

Technical terms are allowed when they help the seller understand the recommendation, but they should be explained in plain language.

The goal is not to impress the buyer with jargon.

The goal is to help the buyer understand:

- what was observed
- why it matters
- what to change or test
- what is uncertain
- what is outside SearchClarity's control

Future idea:

```text
SearchClarity may later build a public glossary/wiki on SearchClarity.co that explains common terms used in reports.
```

If this is built later, report terms may link to glossary pages. This is a future website/content idea, not a launch requirement.

Launch rule:

```text
Do not include glossary/resource links in launch reports or sample PDFs until SearchClarity.co pages are live, stable, and QC-approved.
```

---

## Target Customer

Primary customer:

```text
Etsy sellers who want a professional review of listing visibility and SEO factors without buying a cheap keyword dump.
```

Good-fit buyers:

- handmade sellers
- personalized gift sellers
- digital download sellers
- print-on-demand sellers
- small shops with unclear titles/tags/descriptions
- sellers who want practical recommendations before rewriting listings
- sellers who understand recommendations are not guarantees

Poor-fit buyers:

- buyers demanding guaranteed sales or rankings
- sellers selling counterfeit or trademark-risky products
- sellers who want fake reviews, manipulation, or blackhat tactics
- sellers who refuse to provide usable listing/shop URLs
- sellers who want SearchClarity to log into Etsy for them

---

## Core Deliverable

A PDF report containing:

- scope and methodology
- executive summary
- listing/title/tag/description observations
- keyword/theme observations
- buyer intent and clarity notes
- competitive context observations, where included
- prioritized recommendations
- implementation guidance
- caveats and next steps

The report should be specific enough for the seller to act on without guessing.

## Service Ladder And Companion Gig Rule

The Etsy Listing Visibility Audit is the main diagnostic offer.

SearchClarity may launch a small number of companion gigs near the main audit if they are clearly scoped and support better customer outcomes.

The goal is not to create a pile of shallow add-ons.

The goal is to create a service ladder where each offer has a clear job.

Some companion gigs can be ordered separately. Others work better after the audit because the audit gives the diagnosis, context, and priorities.

### Plain-English Service Types

Use clear public language first. Internal classification can exist underneath.

| Plain-English Type | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Main audit | The primary diagnostic report service | Etsy Listing Visibility Audit |
| Works better after an audit | Can be ordered separately, but is stronger when based on audit findings | Etsy Title and Tag Rewrite |
| Can be ordered separately | Does not require an audit first, but may still benefit from audit context | Etsy Keyword Research Pack |
| Report upgrade | Adds limited depth to the audit package without becoming a full separate service | limited competitor observations |
| Future option | Useful idea, not active until the workflow is ready | short video report walkthrough or post-implementation review |

### Companion Gig / Mini Spec Rule

Every companion gig must have its own mini spec before launch.

No winging it.

Each mini spec should define:

- service name
- who it is for
- whether it works better after an audit or can be ordered separately
- buyer inputs required
- deliverable
- included scope
- out-of-scope work
- pricing status
- revision rule
- QC checks
- relationship to the main audit
- data/record needs for future agent workflows

### Candidate Companion Gigs

Candidate follow-up or related services include:

- Etsy Title and Tag Rewrite
- Etsy Keyword Research Pack
- Etsy Competitor Visibility Observations Report
- Etsy Shop Visibility Report

These may be prepared near launch, but they are not automatically included in the main audit package.

They must be scoped separately.

### Future Options

Future options include:

- short video report walkthrough
- 60-90 Day Post-Implementation Visibility Review

These are not active at launch.

They should be prepared for in the docs/templates where useful, then turned on only when the workflow is ready.

The 60-90 Day Post-Implementation Visibility Review must be a separate future gig or custom-offer candidate, not a gig extra embedded in the original audit order.

Reason:

- Fiverr orders auto-complete after the review window.
- A 60-90 day review needs a new deadline, updated buyer inputs, and separate scope.
- It should compare the updated listing/shop state against the original report after the buyer has had time to implement recommendations.

### Short Video Walkthrough Note

A short video walkthrough means a recorded explanation of the report findings.

Loom is one possible tool for this, but the service feature should be described as a short video report walkthrough rather than by the tool name.

This may become a Premium feature later.

Before activation, SearchClarity must verify:

- Fiverr delivery/link rules
- production time
- privacy risks
- whether the walkthrough stays diagnostic and does not become coaching
- captions or equivalent accessible text option
- internal transcript and transcript QC workflow
- how it affects package pricing

### Fiverr Gallery Video Note

A Fiverr gallery/intro video is a marketing proof asset, not the same as a customer-specific report walkthrough.

A gallery video may become the primary cover media in Fiverr search/results.

Do not use a gallery video unless it is polished enough to outperform the main static gallery image.

If used, it should show the sample report and report structure, not vague marketing fluff.

### Guardrail

Do not bury full future services inside the main audit package.

The main audit should remain a focused diagnostic report:

```text
observe
explain
recommend
prioritize
caveat
```

It should not become:

```text
audit + rewrite + keyword pack + competitor report + implementation coaching + video walkthrough
```

That would create scope creep and reduce report quality.

- product positioning clarity
- attribute/category observations if visible
- main image/thumbnail clarity notes if visibly relevant

Mention main image or thumbnail clarity only if it obviously affects listing understanding.

Do not turn the audit into product photography consulting.

## Recommendation Standard

Major recommendations should generally use this structure:

```text
Observation:
Why it matters:
Recommendation:
How to implement:
Caveat:
```

Recommendations must be:

- specific
- actionable
- tied to the observed listing/shop context
- free of fake precision
- caveated where platform outcomes are uncertain
- compliant with the outcome-guarantee language policy

---

## Caveat Standard

Every report must include caveats that explain:

- Etsy rankings and sales are not guaranteed
- recommendations are based on visible factors and available information
- marketplace behavior can change
- buyer behavior, product fit, pricing, reviews, shipping, and photos also affect performance
- the seller is responsible for deciding what to implement
- launch reports do not link to SearchClarity glossary/resource pages until those pages are live, stable, and QC-approved

---

## Revision And Correction Rule

Each package includes one clarification revision within the original scope.

A customer revision may include:

- clarification of wording
- explanation of a recommendation
- correction of unclear phrasing
- adjustment within the originally purchased scope

A customer revision does not include:

- reviewing new listings
- rewriting listings
- implementing changes inside Etsy
- adding new competitor research
- adding a full keyword research pack
- ongoing support
- coaching

### SearchClarity Mistake Corrections

If SearchClarity made a mistake, fixing it does not count against the buyer's included revision.

Examples:

- wrong listing reviewed
- promised section missing
- PDF export broken
- incorrect URL/reference
- formatting issue that makes the report hard to read
- typo/error that changes meaning

SearchClarity corrections are quality fixes, not customer revision usage.

## Raw Signal Capture

During the audit, analysts may notice generalizable patterns such as:

- repeated weak title structure in a niche
- unclear buyer language patterns
- keyword clusters with strong intent
- competitor listing gaps
- thumbnail/image clarity patterns worth researching later
- product category opportunities
- AI/search readiness gaps

SearchClarity captures raw signals only.

SearchClarity does not score opportunities.

Customer-identifying details must be removed from generalized notes.
