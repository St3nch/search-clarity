# New Service Onboarding Checklist

## Status

```text
draft
```

## Purpose

This checklist defines how SearchClarity adds a new paid service, gig, report, or offer without creating operational chaos.

It is reusable for future SearchClarity services.

It is SearchClarity-owned.

It is not Neon Ronin doctrine.

---

## Core Rule

```text
No new SearchClarity service should be published until the offer, intake, fulfillment, QC, pricing, records, risks, and delivery process are defined.
```

A service idea is not a sellable service until it passes this checklist.

---

## When To Use This Checklist

Use this checklist when SearchClarity wants to add or materially change:

- a Fiverr gig
- an Upwork project
- a direct website service
- a report product
- an audit package
- a rewrite/editing service
- a keyword research pack
- a strategy plan
- a new delivery tier
- a major add-on or extra

---

## Stage 1 - Idea Capture

Answer:

- What is the service called?
- Who is it for?
- What buyer problem does it solve?
- What does the customer receive?
- Why would someone buy this instead of a cheap keyword dump?
- Does this service fit SearchClarity's brand?
- Is this a new service or a repackaged version of an existing one?

Output:

```text
one-paragraph service idea summary
```

---

## Stage 4 - Buyer Intake Design

Define what the buyer must provide before work starts.

Questions:

- What URL(s), files, screenshots, or context are required?
- What is mandatory vs optional?
- What can be asked after purchase?
- What must never be requested?
- What happens if intake is incomplete?
- Does intake expose sensitive data?
- Does the service require credentials? If yes, can it be redesigned to avoid them?

Output:

```text
buyer intake question set
```

---

## Stage 5 - Data And Observation Requirements

Define what SearchClarity needs to review.

Examples:

- public listing/page observations
- keyword observations
- marketplace search observations
- competitor observations
- customer-supplied screenshots
- published platform guidance
- public SERP observations
- future third-party tool data, if later approved

For each data category, decide:

| Data / observation | Required? | Source | Private? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |  |

Rules:

- Prefer public/manual observations first.
- Do not require customer credentials unless absolutely unavoidable.
- Do not store unnecessary sensitive data.
- Do not invent exact data precision if the source is only directional.

---

## Stage 6 - Deliverable Template

Define the deliverable.

Questions:

- Is the output a PDF, Markdown report, spreadsheet, Loom, checklist, or bundle?
- What sections must it contain?
- What evidence/reasoning is required?
- What action plan is included?
- What caveats are required?
- What sample/proof asset is needed before publishing?
- What does a finished deliverable look like?

Output:

```text
deliverable template or outline
```

---

## Stage 7 - Quality Control

Define service-specific QC checks.

Minimum QC questions:

- Does the deliverable match the purchased scope?
- Are all major recommendations specific and actionable?
- Are caveats present?
- Is outcome-guarantee language absent?
- Are customer URLs/files handled correctly?
- Are competitor observations fair and non-copycat?
- Is anything generic filler?
- Is the report professional enough to send?

Output:

```text
service-specific QC checklist
```

---

## Stage 8 - Risk And Hard-No Review

Every service must pass a hard-no review.

Check for:

- ranking guarantees
- sales/revenue guarantees
- trademark-risky keyword bait
- blackhat tactics
- fake reviews
- platform manipulation
- off-platform contact violations
- request for unnecessary credentials
- copying competitor content
- prohibited product categories
- unsafe or misleading claims

Output:

```text
risk notes and required caveats
```

---

## Stage 9 - Pricing And Package Review

Before publishing, pricing must be reconciled against:

```text
docs/operations/pricing/pricing-source-of-truth.md
```

Questions:

- What is the launch price?
- What is the standard price?
- Which tier is the hero tier?
- Does the price reflect production time?
- Does the price attract the right buyer?
- What add-ons are allowed?
- What creates scope creep?

Output:

```text
approved pricing/package table
```

---

## Stage 10 - Fulfillment SOP

Define how the service is fulfilled.

Minimum workflow:

```text
order/request received
-> intake reviewed
-> scope confirmed
-> observations/research completed
-> draft created
-> QC completed
-> final deliverable prepared
-> delivery sent
-> revision handled if needed
-> records updated
-> raw signals captured if relevant
-> order closed
```

Output:

```text
service fulfillment SOP
```

---

## Stage 11 - Records And Signal Capture

Define records created by this service.

Minimum records:

- customer/order/request record
- intake record
- report/deliverable record
- QC record
- delivery record
- revision record, if applicable
- consent record, if public use is allowed
- raw market signal record, if something generalizable is observed

Rules:

- SearchClarity captures raw signals.
- SearchClarity does not score opportunities.
- Customer-identifying information does not belong in generalized signal notes.

---

## Stage 12 - Launch Readiness Decision

A new service is ready to publish only when:

- offer scope is locked
- pricing is locked
- intake questions are written
- deliverable template exists
- sample/proof asset exists, if needed
- QC checklist exists
- fulfillment SOP exists
- hard-no rules are reviewed
- records are defined
- channel-specific requirements are verified
- final copy is reviewed against outcome-guarantee policy

Decision:

```text
ready / not_ready / parked
```

## Non-Goals

This checklist does not:

- define Neon Ronin workspace onboarding
- define agent automation
- define database architecture
- replace channel-specific platform research
- replace service-specific SOPs
- approve publishing by itself
