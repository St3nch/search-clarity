## 4. Executive Summary

Maplewood Candle Co. has a clear product-market direction: gift candles for specific recipients and occasions. The main visibility problem is not the product concept. The main problem is that the listings are not consistently structured around how gift buyers search on Etsy.

### Main findings

| Finding | Why It Matters | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Titles lead with brand-style phrases instead of buyer-intent terms | Etsy buyers usually search by recipient, occasion, and product type, not by cute phrase first | Do first |
| Tags repeat broad words already present in titles | Repeated broad tags waste tag slots that could cover recipient and occasion angles | Do first |
| Recipient and occasion modifiers are missing or buried | The shop sells giftable products, but many listings do not clearly target the exact gift buyer | Do next |

### Primary opportunity

The shop's strongest opportunity is to move from broad product titles like `Candle Gift` and `Soy Candle 8oz` toward buyer-intent structures like:

> `Funny Retirement Candle for Coworker - Office Retirement Gift for Boss or Colleague`

This does not change the product. It changes which buyer the product is positioned to reach.

### Recommended path

Fix Listings 1 and 2 first. They have the clearest gift-buyer intent and the strongest opportunity for better title/tag alignment. Then apply the same structure to Listings 3 and 4. Listing 5 should either be converted into a specific gift/occasion listing or retired as a low-signal category filler.

## 6. Listing 1 Audit - Smells Like Retirement - Candle Gift

### Current title

`Smells Like Retirement - Candle Gift`

### Diagnosis

This title has a memorable phrase, but it is not using enough of the title to reach a specific buyer. The phrase `Smells Like Retirement` is playful, but Etsy buyers are more likely to search for `retirement gift for coworker`, `funny retirement candle`, `boss retirement gift`, or `office retirement gift`.

### Title issue

**Observation:** The title leads with a cute phrase and ends with the generic phrase `Candle Gift`. It does not name the recipient, the gift context, or the workplace angle.

**Why it matters:** `Candle Gift` is broad. It competes against too many unrelated candle listings. `Retirement gift for coworker` and `funny retirement candle` are more specific and carry clearer purchase intent.

**Recommendation:** Move the strongest buyer-intent phrase to the front, then keep the funny phrase as secondary positioning.

**How to implement:** Rewrite in the direction of:

`Funny Retirement Candle for Coworker - Office Retirement Gift for Boss or Colleague`

**Caveat:** This is a title direction, not a guaranteed ranking formula. Test for 14 to 21 days before making another major title change.

### Tag issue

**Observation:** A likely tag pattern for this listing would overuse broad terms like `retirement`, `candle`, `gift`, and `funny gift`.

**Why it matters:** Single-word or very broad tags compete against too many listings and do not add much coverage if the same words already appear in the title.

**Recommendation:** Replace broad tags with long-tail recipient and occasion phrases.

**How to implement:** Test tag directions such as:

- `funny retirement candle`
- `retirement gift coworker`
- `office retirement gift`
- `boss retirement gift`
- `colleague retirement gift`
- `last day work gift`
- `retirement party gift`
- `funny coworker gift`
- `going away gift`
- `retiring coworker`

**Caveat:** Do not add recipient terms that the product does not genuinely fit. If the candle is designed only for coworkers, do not stuff in `teacher`, `nurse`, or `dad` just because those terms have search demand.

### Description issue

**Observation:** A description that repeats the title without adding buyer context misses an opportunity to explain who the candle is for and when to give it.

**Why it matters:** The first paragraph helps both the buyer and search systems understand the listing. Repeating the title wastes space.

**Recommendation:** Open with a sentence that names the recipient, occasion, and gift intent.

**How to implement:** Use a description opening in this direction:

> A funny retirement candle for a coworker leaving the office - a lighthearted last-day gift for a colleague, boss, or work friend who is finally calling it a career.

**Caveat:** Avoid comma-separated keyword lists. The description should read naturally.

### One-line verdict

This listing is not far off. It has a good product angle, but the title needs to lead with the buyer's search, not the joke.

## 7. Listing 2 Audit - For the Best Nurse - Soy Candle 8oz

### Current title

`For the Best Nurse - Soy Candle 8oz`

### Diagnosis

This listing has a clear recipient, but the title wastes early space on a phrase buyers are less likely to search exactly. The product detail `Soy Candle 8oz` is useful, but it should not outrank the buyer's reason for purchasing.

### Title issue

**Observation:** The title starts with `For the Best Nurse`, which reads like product copy rather than search language. `Soy Candle 8oz` appears early even though material and size are secondary search signals for this gift category.

**Why it matters:** Nurse gift buyers often search by occasion and relationship: `nurse appreciation gift`, `thank you nurse gift`, `RN graduation gift`, `nurse retirement gift`, or `gift for nurse coworker`.

**Recommendation:** Lead with the gift intent and recipient. Move material and size details into the description or later title wording.

**How to implement:** Rewrite in the direction of:

`Nurse Appreciation Gift Candle - Thank You Nurse Gift, RN Coworker Soy Candle`

**Caveat:** Use `RN` only if the product is appropriate for registered nurses generally. Avoid medical specialty terms such as ICU, ER, or labor and delivery unless the design actually fits that buyer.

### Tag issue

**Observation:** The current structure likely underuses occasion terms such as appreciation, thank you, graduation, retirement, and coworker gift.

**Why it matters:** Recipient-only targeting can be too broad. Occasion terms narrow the listing toward a buyer who is ready to purchase.

**Recommendation:** Build the tag mix around recipient + occasion + gift format.

**How to implement:** Test tag directions such as:

- `nurse appreciation gift`
- `thank you nurse gift`
- `gift for nurse`
- `nurse coworker gift`
- `rn graduation gift`
- `nurse retirement gift`
- `nurse candle gift`
- `nurse week gift`
- `medical worker gift`
- `soy candle gift`

**Caveat:** Do not use hospital names, medical brand names, or protected slogans as keyword bait.

### Description issue

**Observation:** If the description leads with scent or candle specs, it delays the gift context.

**Why it matters:** Gift buyers want to know whether the product fits their recipient and occasion before they care about burn time or scent notes.

**Recommendation:** Put the gifting use case first, then scent and material details second.

**How to implement:** Open in this direction:

> A thoughtful nurse appreciation candle for an RN, nurse coworker, or healthcare friend who deserves a simple thank-you gift after a long shift.

**Caveat:** Keep medical claims out of the listing. Do not imply therapeutic benefits from the candle.

### One-line verdict

This listing has a strong recipient, but it needs an occasion and gift-intent frame to reach higher-intent searches.

## 8. Listing 3 Audit - Teacher Gift Candle Personalized Lavender Eucalyptus Relaxing Self Care Present

### Current title

`Teacher Gift Candle Personalized Lavender Eucalyptus Relaxing Self Care Present`

### Diagnosis

This title tries to do too many jobs at once. It targets teacher gift, personalization, scent, relaxation, self-care, and generic present language. The result is hard to scan and does not give Etsy a clean primary intent.

### Title issue

**Observation:** The title includes several valid ideas, but they are not ordered by buyer intent. `Teacher Gift Candle` is strong. `Personalized` is strong. `Lavender Eucalyptus Relaxing Self Care Present` is too much supporting language in the primary title.

**Why it matters:** Etsy titles need a clear hierarchy. When every phrase appears equally important, the listing becomes less focused.

**Recommendation:** Lead with personalized teacher gift + occasion, then use scent as a secondary detail.

**How to implement:** Rewrite in the direction of:

`Personalized Teacher Gift Candle - End of Year Teacher Appreciation Gift, Lavender Soy Candle`

**Caveat:** `End of Year` is seasonal. If this listing sells year-round, rotate the title seasonally or use `Teacher Appreciation Gift` as the evergreen lead.

### Tag issue

**Observation:** A stuffed title often leads to a stuffed tag set. The likely missing angles are school-year timing, teacher appreciation week, thank-you gifts, and personalized name gifts.

**Why it matters:** Teacher gifts have seasonal peaks. Missing seasonal tags means the listing may underperform during the exact months when buyers are most active.

**Recommendation:** Split the tag strategy into evergreen teacher terms and seasonal teacher terms.

**How to implement:** Test tag directions such as:

- `personalized teacher gift`
- `teacher gift candle`
- `teacher appreciation gift`
- `end of year teacher gift`
- `teacher thank you gift`
- `teacher name gift`
- `gift for teacher`
- `appreciation week gift`
- `last day school gift`
- `lavender candle gift`

**Caveat:** Do not run seasonal terms all year without checking whether they still fit the buying moment.

### Description issue

**Observation:** The title implies personalization, but the description must make the personalization process clear quickly.

**Why it matters:** Personalized products lose buyers when instructions are buried. A buyer should immediately know what name or message they need to provide.

**Recommendation:** Put personalization instructions in the first paragraph or immediately after the opening sentence.

**How to implement:** Add copy in this direction:

> Add the teacher's name, class year, or a short thank-you message at checkout. This candle works as an end-of-year teacher gift, teacher appreciation week gift, or simple classroom thank-you.

**Caveat:** Keep personalization limits clear. If only names fit, do not imply long custom messages are available.

### One-line verdict

This listing has strong ingredients, but the title needs hierarchy. Simplify the title and let the description carry the scent and self-care details.

## 9. Listing 4 Audit - Funny Office Candle - Coworker Birthday Gift

### Current title

`Funny Office Candle - Coworker Birthday Gift`

### Diagnosis

This is the cleanest current title in the set. It has humor, workplace context, recipient, and occasion. The missed opportunity is specificity. The title does not say whether the candle is personalized, whether it fits a boss or coworker, or whether it is meant for office humor, promotion, leaving job, or birthday.

### Title issue

**Observation:** `Funny Office Candle` is useful but broad. `Coworker Birthday Gift` is stronger buyer intent and should be closer to the front.

**Why it matters:** Buyers searching for coworker birthday gifts are closer to buying than buyers searching for general office candles.

**Recommendation:** Lead with coworker birthday gift and keep funny office candle as a modifier.

**How to implement:** Rewrite in the direction of:

`Coworker Birthday Gift Candle - Funny Office Candle for Work Friend or Boss`

**Caveat:** Use `boss` only if the humor is appropriate for a boss. Some funny coworker products do not translate upward in workplace hierarchy.

### Tag issue

**Observation:** This listing can cover multiple workplace gift angles, but the tag set should not become a random office-gift dump.

**Why it matters:** Workplace gift terms split by recipient and occasion. Birthday, promotion, thank-you, and leaving-job are different searches.

**Recommendation:** Keep this listing focused on birthday. Use separate listings for promotion or leaving-job gifts if those are real product angles.

**How to implement:** Test tag directions such as:

- `coworker birthday gift`
- `funny office candle`
- `work friend gift`
- `boss birthday gift`
- `office birthday gift`
- `funny coworker gift`
- `desk gift coworker`
- `employee birthday gift`
- `office humor gift`
- `gift for colleague`

**Caveat:** Do not use the same tag set for retirement, birthday, promotion, and going-away listings. Each occasion deserves its own signal.

### Description issue

**Observation:** The description should clarify tone. Funny office gifts can be sarcastic, sweet, rude, or safe-for-work.

**Why it matters:** Buyers hesitate when humor tone is unclear. Clear tone helps conversion, which indirectly supports visibility over time.

**Recommendation:** State the humor style in plain language.

**How to implement:** Add a first-paragraph sentence like:

> A safe-for-work funny candle for a coworker birthday, office celebration, or work friend who appreciates light humor without anything too rude.

**Caveat:** If the candle is rude or explicit, say that clearly instead. Misleading tone can hurt reviews.

### One-line verdict

This listing is close. The highest-value change is moving `Coworker Birthday Gift` to the front and tightening the tags around the birthday occasion.

## 10. Listing 5 Audit - Candle - 4oz Travel Tin - Natural Soy - Multiple Scents

### Current title

`Candle - 4oz Travel Tin - Natural Soy - Multiple Scents`

### Diagnosis

This listing is written like a product catalog entry, not an Etsy gift listing. It tells the buyer what the item is made of, but not why someone would buy it, who it is for, or when it would be given.

### Title issue

**Observation:** The title contains only product specifications: candle, size, tin format, material, and scent options.

**Why it matters:** Product-spec terms are useful after the buyer has already chosen the product category. Etsy gift buyers often search by purpose first: stocking stuffer, bridesmaid gift, travel gift, thank-you gift, wedding favor, or small gift.

**Recommendation:** Decide what job this listing should do. Either turn it into a specific gift listing or treat it as a low-priority filler listing.

**How to implement:** Choose one positioning path:

- Gift path: `Mini Soy Candle Gift - 4oz Travel Tin Candle, Small Thank You Gift or Stocking Stuffer`
- Event path: `Mini Candle Wedding Favor - 4oz Soy Travel Tin, Custom Scent Party Favor`
- Self-care path: `Travel Tin Soy Candle - Small Self Care Gift, Mini Candle for Desk or Bath`

**Caveat:** Do not try to target all three paths in one title. If the product serves all three, create separate listings only if the photos, description, and positioning are meaningfully different.

### Tag issue

**Observation:** Current product-spec terms likely attract browsers, not gift buyers.

**Why it matters:** Broad product tags like `soy candle` and `travel candle` are crowded. Gift-specific tags are more likely to convert.

**Recommendation:** Build tags around the chosen path, not around every possible candle use.

**How to implement:** For the gift path, test tags such as:

- `mini soy candle`
- `small thank you gift`
- `stocking stuffer gift`
- `travel tin candle`
- `small candle gift`
- `coworker small gift`
- `bridesmaid candle`
- `party favor candle`
- `teacher small gift`
- `desk candle gift`

**Caveat:** If the listing stays generic, do not spend much optimization time on it. The higher-return work is in the recipient and occasion listings.

### Description issue

**Observation:** A generic description will not save a generic title. The listing needs a clear use case.

**Why it matters:** Buyers need to understand why this candle exists in the shop. Is it a favor? A stocking stuffer? A travel candle? A sample size?

**Recommendation:** Rewrite the first paragraph to match the chosen positioning path.

**How to implement:** For the gift path:

> A small 4oz soy candle gift for thank-you baskets, stocking stuffers, coworker gifts, or simple self-care moments. Choose from multiple scents and send as an easy under-$20 gift.

**Caveat:** If the product is not priced under $20, remove that claim.

### One-line verdict

This listing should not be optimized as-is. Pick a buyer use case first, then rewrite the title and tags around that use case.

## 11. Title Structure Analysis

| Listing | Current Title Pattern | Main Problem | Recommended Direction | Reason |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cute phrase + generic gift | Buyer intent missing | Funny retirement candle for coworker | Stronger recipient/occasion targeting |
| 2 | Praise phrase + material/spec | Occasion missing | Nurse appreciation gift candle | Matches gift-buyer searches |
| 3 | Many keywords with no hierarchy | Stuffed and hard to scan | Personalized teacher gift candle | Cleaner primary intent |
| 4 | Product/context + recipient occasion | Good but order can improve | Coworker birthday gift candle | Moves strongest phrase forward |
| 5 | Product specs only | No buyer intent | Mini soy candle gift or wedding favor | Needs a use case |

### Recommended title pattern for this shop

Use this structure for most gift listings:

> [Recipient or occasion gift phrase] + [product type] + [specific angle] + [secondary recipient/occasion]

Examples:

- `Funny Retirement Candle for Coworker - Office Retirement Gift for Boss or Colleague`
- `Nurse Appreciation Gift Candle - Thank You Nurse Gift, RN Coworker Soy Candle`
- `Personalized Teacher Gift Candle - End of Year Teacher Appreciation Gift`

### What to avoid

- Starting with cute phrases unless the phrase itself is searched.
- Repeating the same core word three or more times.
- Using product specs before buyer intent.
- Stuffing unrelated occasions into one title.
- Adding trademarked brand names or protected slogans.

## 12. Tag Coverage Analysis

| Listing | Current Tag Pattern | Missing Keyword Angles | Recommended Tag Direction |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Likely broad retirement/candle/funny tags | Coworker, boss, office, last day, going away | Long-tail retirement recipient tags |
| 2 | Likely nurse + soy candle terms | Appreciation, thank you, nurse week, RN graduation | Recipient + occasion tags |
| 3 | Likely teacher + scent + self-care terms | End of year, appreciation week, personalized name | Seasonal teacher gift tags |
| 4 | Likely funny office/coworker terms | Birthday, work friend, boss, colleague | Workplace birthday tags |
| 5 | Likely soy candle/travel tin terms | Small gift, stocking stuffer, favor, thank-you | Choose one path and tag around it |

### Tag strategy principles

The shop should use tags to expand coverage, not repeat the same words from the title. A useful 13-tag set usually mixes:

- 3 to 4 recipient or audience tags.
- 3 to 4 occasion tags.
- 2 to 3 product-type tags.
- 2 to 3 long-tail composite tags.
- 1 to 2 style, material, or use-case tags.

### Shop-wide tag warning

Do not use the same 13 tags across every listing. It is tempting because the products are all candles, but Etsy needs listing-specific signals. A retirement candle, nurse candle, teacher candle, and coworker birthday candle should not share the same tag set except for a few broad product terms.

## 14. How We Reached These Recommendations

SearchClarity's process for this sample followed the same reasoning structure used in paid reports.

1. Reviewed all five listing titles manually for buyer-intent clarity.
2. Checked whether each title led with recipient, occasion, product type, or a brand/cute phrase.
3. Identified repeated pattern: product/cute wording appears before buyer search intent.
4. Grouped listings into recipient and occasion clusters: retirement, nurse, teacher, coworker birthday, generic candle.
5. Built a sample keyword shortlist for each cluster using illustrative eRank, Etsy Marketplace Insights, Etsy search observation, Google Trends, and Pinterest Trends references.
6. Checked keyword directions for obvious IP risk, including brand names, protected slogans, character names, and misleading comparison terms.
7. Rewrote title directions around buyer intent, not around maximum keyword stuffing.
8. Built tag directions to add coverage that the title does not already carry.
9. Wrote description-opening recommendations to clarify gift use case and personalization.
10. Prioritized actions by likely impact and ease of implementation.

### Reasoning chain

The shop does not need to become more generic to reach more buyers. It needs to become more specific in the way buyers already search.

A broad phrase like `candle gift` reaches too many possible buyers and too many competitors. A phrase like `funny retirement candle for coworker` reaches fewer people, but those people are much closer to buying the exact type of product Maplewood Candle Co. sells.

## 15. Recommended Path and Alternatives

### Primary recommended path

Restructure all five listings around recipient and occasion search intent, starting with Listings 1 and 2.

**Why this path:** It keeps the product line intact while improving how each listing communicates with Etsy search and gift buyers. It is also low-risk because the seller can test two listings before applying the pattern to the whole shop.

### Alternative A - Conservative cleanup path

Fix only Listing 1 and Listing 2 first. Track Etsy impressions, visits, and favorites for 21 to 30 days. If the changes improve visibility or engagement, apply the same structure to Listings 3 and 4.

**Best for:** Sellers who are nervous about changing too many titles at once.

**Trade-off:** Slower learning across the shop.

### Alternative B - Growth test path

Apply the title and tag fixes to all five listings, then add two new sub-niche listings targeting stronger long-tail buyer intent:

- Personalized nurse retirement candle.
- End-of-year personalized teacher candle.

**Best for:** Sellers who can create or adapt products quickly and want to test new buyer-intent niches.

**Trade-off:** Higher workload and more moving parts to measure.
